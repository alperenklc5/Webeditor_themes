Frequently Asked Questions

Therapy can benefit anyone facing life challenges, stress, anxiety, depression, relationship issues, or simply seeking personal growth. You don't need to be in crisis to benefit from therapy—it's a proactive step toward better mental health and self-understanding.

Yes, we accept most major insurance plans including Blue Cross, Aetna, Cigna, and UnitedHealthcare. We also offer sliding scale fees for those without insurance. Please contact us to verify your specific coverage and discuss payment options.

Your first session is about getting to know each other. We'll discuss what brought you to therapy, your goals, and any concerns you have. This is a judgment-free space where you can share openly. Together, we'll create a treatment plan tailored to your needs.

The duration varies based on individual needs and goals. Some clients find relief in a few sessions, while others benefit from longer-term therapy. We'll regularly assess your progress and adjust the treatment plan as needed. You're always in control of your therapeutic journey.

Yes, confidentiality is a cornerstone of therapy. Everything you share is protected by law and professional ethics. The only exceptions are if there's a risk of harm to yourself or others, or if required by court order. Your privacy and trust are paramount.

About Me

Dr. Jennifer Matthews

I'm a licensed clinical psychologist with over 12 years of experience helping individuals, couples, and families navigate life's challenges. My approach is warm, collaborative, and evidence-based, drawing from cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT), mindfulness practices, and person-centered therapy.

I specialize in anxiety, depression, trauma, relationship issues, and life transitions. My goal is to create a safe, non-judgmental space where you can explore your thoughts and feelings, develop coping strategies, and work toward meaningful change.
